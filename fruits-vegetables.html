Fruits and vegetables are an indispensable part of any diet, not only for vegetarians, but also for non-vegetarians. Special emphasis is being paid to the consumption of fresh fruits and vegetable, as they are the important sources of essential minerals and vitamins.

MANGO

Botanical Name - Mangifera indica

Luscious and sweet, mango is known as "the king of fruit". Mangoes are now one of the most popular fruits crops in tropical and subtropical areas of the world. In addition to their juicy tropical flavor, mangos deliver nutritional value and make healthy eating a joyful and nostalgic experience.

Nutritional Value per 100gm

Nutrient Amount
Energy (Kcal) 69
Carbohydrate 25 g
Protein 1.40 g
Fiber 2.6 g
Sodium 60 mg
Vitamin C 2 mg
Vitamin E 1.5 mg

Potato:

Botanical Name - Solanum tuberosum

The potato is a crop which has always been the ‘poor man’s friend’. Potatoes are a rich source of starch, vitamins especially C and B1 and minerals. Commercially it is used for the production of starch and alcohol. As a food product itself, potatoes are converted into dried products such as ‘potato chips’, ‘sliced’ or ‘shredded potatoes’.

Nutrient Amount
Energy (Kcal) 77
Carbohydrate 17 g
Protein 2 g
Fiber 2.2 g
Sodium 6 mg
Potassium 421 mg

Onion :

Botanical Name - Allium cepa L

Onion is commonly known as “Queen of the kitchen” due to its highly valued ﬂavor, aroma, and unique taste. It helps in improving the bone health, lowering cholesterol, enhancing skin glow and regulating insulin levels. Commercially it can be used as onion paste, dehydrated onion flakes, onion powder, onion oil, onion vinegar, and beverages etc.

Nutrient Amount
Energy (Kcal) 40
Carbohydrate 9 g
Protein 1.1 g
Fiber 1.7 g
Sodium 6 mg
Potassium 146 mg
