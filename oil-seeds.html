Oil Seeds

Description:

Oilseeds are high-value agricultural commodity, which attracted more attention for its vegetable oils, livestock feed, pharmaceuticals, and other chemical industrial uses. Well-known edible oilseeds are Groundnut, Rapeseed & Mustard, Sesame, Soybean, Sunflower.

Groundnut

Botanical Name - Arachis hypogaea

The peanut, also known as the groundnut, goober, monkey nut, is grown mainly for its edible seeds. As a health perspective it helps in lowing blood sugars, boost immunity and improve the functioning of brain. Commercially it is used for making peanut butter, backed products, fertilizer and cattle feed industry.

Nutritional Value per 100gm

Energy (Kcal) 567
Protein 26 g
Carbohydrate 16 g
Fiber 9 g
Calcium 134 mg
Iron 6.7 mg
Oil Content(%) 40 - 55 %

Sesame:

Botanical Name - Sesamum indicum

Sesame seed is a common ingredient in cooking for its rich, nutty flavor. The seeds are rich source of protein, thiamin and vitamin B6, which helps in promoting digestion, bone health, and controlling bad cholesterol levels. Its non-culinary application includes its use as an ingredient in soap, cosmetics, lubricants and medicines.

Energy (Kcal) 573
Protein 17.7 g
Carbohydrate 23.4 g
Fiber 11.8 g
Calcium 975 mg
Iron 14.6 mg
Oil Content(%) 50-60 %
